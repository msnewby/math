Comparing Numbers
with >, =, <

Which number is bigger? Which is smaller? Are they the same?

🔢 2nd Grade Skill
👥 Small Group
⏱ 30 Minutes
🔢 Lesson 3 of 100
🐊 Meet the HUNGRY ALLIGATOR!
He ALWAYS eats the BIGGER number!
>
GREATER THAN
Mouth open → bigger number
5 > 3 → "5 is greater than 3"
=
EQUAL TO
Both sides are the SAME
4 = 4 → "4 equals 4"
<
LESS THAN
Mouth open ← bigger number
3 < 5 → "3 is less than 5"
🧠 Memory trick: The open mouth ALWAYS faces the BIGGER number — the alligator is hungry for the most food!
📖 Words We Need to Know
Compare
Look at two numbers to decide which is bigger, smaller, or equal
Greater Than >
The first number is BIGGER
327 > 198
Less Than <
The first number is SMALLER
474 < 557
Equal To =
Both numbers are the SAME
305 = 305
Place Value
Hundreds, Tens, Ones — always compare hundreds FIRST!
🏆 Always start with the HUNDREDS place — if they're different, you already have your answer!
👀 Watch Me — 327 vs 198
327

198

Tens: 2 vs 9

327 > 198 ✅ "327 is greater than 198"
1

Look at the HUNDREDS first

327 has 3 hundreds. 198 has 1 hundred. 3 > 1 — done!

2

If hundreds are equal — check TENS

Only move to tens if the hundreds digits are the same number.

3

If tens are equal — check ONES

Only move to ones if hundreds AND tens are both equal.
