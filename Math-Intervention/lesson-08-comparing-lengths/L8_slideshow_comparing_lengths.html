Comparing Lengths
How Much Longer?

Measure two objects. Subtract. Find the difference!
📏 2nd Grade Skill
👥 Small Group
⏱ 30 Minutes
🔢 Lesson 8 of 100
📏 A pencil is 12 inches.
A crayon is 6 inches.
How much LONGER is the pencil?
Pencil
12 inches
12 in.
Crayon
6 inches
6 in.
12 - 6 =
6 inches longer!
💡 "How much longer?" means SUBTRACT the shorter length from the longer one!
📖 Words We Need to Know
Longer
A greater length — reaches further than the other object
Shorter
A smaller length — doesn't reach as far
Difference
How much MORE one measurement is than the other — found by subtracting
Compare
Look at two lengths and decide which is longer, shorter, or the same
How Much Longer
Subtract! Longer length MINUS shorter length = the difference
📐 KEY FORMULA: Longer length − Shorter length = Difference (how much longer)
👀 Watch Me — Pencil vs Crayon!
Pencil ✏️
12 in.
Crayon 🖍️
6 in.
6 in. more!
12 − 6 = 6 inches ✅
1

Measure BOTH objects

Pencil = 12 inches. Crayon = 6 inches.

2

Which is LONGER?

The pencil is longer — 12 > 6. So pencil goes first in the equation.

3

SUBTRACT to find the difference

12 − 6 = 6. The pencil is 6 inches longer!

✏️

Write a complete answer

The pencil is 6 inches longer than the crayon.

🖐️ Let's Use Our Hands!
📏

Measure & Subtract

Grab 2 objects from your desk. Measure both. Write the subtraction equation to find the difference!

🧱

Linking Cubes

Build a tower of 9 cubes and one of 5 cubes. How many more does the taller tower have?

📜

Paper Strips

Cut two strips — one 8 inches, one 5 inches. Hold them next to each other. Count the gap!

✏️

Draw & Compare

Draw two lines of different lengths. Label each one. Write the subtraction to find the difference.

⚠️ Always SUBTRACT the shorter from the longer — never the other way around!
