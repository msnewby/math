📮 Laila has 14 stamps. She uses 3 to mail letters. Then she uses 5 more for a big letter. How many stamps does she NOW have?

Step 1 — first event

14 − 3 = 11
She mails 3 letters first
→

Step 2 — second event

11 − 5 = 6
Then uses 5 more
→

Final answer

6 stamps ✅
14 → 11 → 6
💡 TWO things happen in the story — you need TWO equations to solve it!
📖 Words We Need to Know
Two-Step Problem
A word problem that needs TWO math steps to solve
Step 1
The FIRST thing that happens — solve this equation first
Step 2
The SECOND thing that happens — start with Step 1's answer
Uses / Spent
Signal words for SUBTRACT — she "uses" stamps means take away
Show Your Work
Write BOTH equations — 14−3=11 then 11−5=6
🚦 "Uses" / "spends" / "gives away" / "eats" = SUBTRACT each time! Watch for TWO of them!

🧠 Strategies That Help!
🔵🔵

Two Number Lines

Line 1: Start at 14, jump back 3 = land on 11. Line 2: Start at 11, jump back 5 = land on 6!

📮

Draw & Cross Out

Draw 14 stamps. Cross out 3. Count leftovers. Then cross out 5 more. Count again!

📝

Two Equations

Write both equations clearly. Draw a box around Step 1's answer — that's Step 2's start!

🔵

Counters

Put out 14 counters. Remove 3. Count what's left. Remove 5 more. Count the final amount!

⚠️ COMMON MISTAKE: Don't start Step 2 with 14! Step 2 ALWAYS starts with Step 1's answer!
⚠️ Watch Out for This Mistake!
❌ WRONG!
Step 1: 14 − 3 = 11
Step 2: 14 − 5 = 9 ❌
(started Step 2 with 14 again!)
✅ RIGHT!
Step 1: 14 − 3 = 11
Step 2: 11 − 5 = 6 ✅
(used Step 1's answer!)
🔑 KEY RULE: Step 2 starts where Step 1 LEFT OFF — not where the story STARTED!
